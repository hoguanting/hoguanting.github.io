Side projects usually die from constant reinterpretation. Every week they become a new framework experiment, a new product concept, or a new design exercise. Momentum disappears because the target keeps moving.

The most useful constraint is to define what counts as finished before the fun part begins. Once that line exists, you can make tradeoffs instead of restarting the idea every weekend.
