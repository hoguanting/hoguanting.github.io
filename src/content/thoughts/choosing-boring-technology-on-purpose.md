Most teams do not fail because their stack is too ordinary. They fail because they underestimate the cost of novelty. Boring technology is often valuable precisely because other people have already paid for the mistakes, edge cases, and operational lessons.

Choosing a familiar tool is not the same thing as lacking ambition. It is often the more ambitious move because it preserves attention for product quality, performance, and user experience.
