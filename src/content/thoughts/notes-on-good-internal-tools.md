Good internal tools are usually quiet. They reduce repeated work, make the correct path obvious, and avoid forcing teams to learn a private framework just to complete routine tasks.

The test I like is simple: does this tool reduce coordination overhead for the people using it every week? If the answer is unclear, the tool is probably optimized for its builder rather than its users.
